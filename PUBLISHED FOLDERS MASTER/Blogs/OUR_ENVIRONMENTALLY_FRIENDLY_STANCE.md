### Using FSC Paper and Soy Ink for Packaging

One of the first steps we took toward environmentally responsible packaging was the transition to FSC-certified paper. FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) certification ensures that the paper we use comes from responsibly managed forests that meet rigorous environmental and social standards. By choosing FSC-certified paper, we are actively contributing to the preservation of forests, protecting biodiversity, and supporting sustainable forestry practices.

In addition to using FSC paper, we have also made the switch to soy ink for all of our printed materials. Traditional ink is often made with petroleum-based solvents and chemicals, which can be harmful to the environment. Soy ink, on the other hand, is made from soybean oil and is biodegradable, making it a much more eco-friendly alternative. Soy ink also has lower levels of volatile organic compounds (VOCs), which means it has less of an impact on air quality.

These choices represent just the beginning of our commitment to environmentally friendly practices. They may seem like small steps, but they are an essential part of our broader efforts to operate in a more sustainable way and minimize our environmental impact. By using FSC-certified paper and soy ink, we are not only reducing our carbon footprint but also setting a standard for other companies to follow in embracing eco-friendly materials.

### Introducing Recycled Paper Pulp Packaging

While our early packaging was made with materials that were less than ideal for the environment, we are proud to announce a major breakthrough in our sustainability efforts. We have found a way to produce recycled paper pulp packaging that protects the precious contents of our products while also being eco-friendly. This new material is made from 100% recycled paper fibers, which means that it reduces the need for virgin paper production and helps divert waste from landfills.

The beauty of using recycled paper pulp is that it is biodegradable, compostable, and recyclable—offering a more sustainable option than traditional plastic or non-recyclable materials. The moldable nature of recycled paper pulp allows us to create protective packaging that fits the specific shape and size of our products, reducing the amount of packaging needed and ensuring that our products are protected during transit.

By switching to recycled paper pulp for our packaging, we have made a significant step forward in reducing our environmental impact. Not only does this new packaging material align with our eco-friendly values, but it also provides our customers with peace of mind knowing that they are supporting a brand that is dedicated to reducing waste and protecting the environment.
