## Anatomy & Physiology

### Anatomy of the Liver

The liver is the body's largest internal organ and is located in the upper right quadrant of the abdomen.

#### Physical Structure

**Size & Function Diversity:**
- Has anatomical and functional diversity
- Carrying out over 500 different functions
- Includes metabolism, detoxification, regulation of hormone secretion

**Lobes & Organization:**
- Comprises two lobes
- Each divided into smaller lobules

**Blood Supply:**
- Hepatic artery takes oxygenated blood from heart to liver
- Portal vein supplies liver with nutrient-rich blood from digestive system
- Hepatic vein drains blood from liver and carries it back to heart
- Receives innervation from autonomic nervous system

### Physiology of the Liver

The liver is responsible for a variety of vital functions.

#### Key Functions

**1. Metabolism**
- Metabolizes carbohydrates, lipids, and proteins
- Uses enzymes to convert substances into usable forms

**2. Detoxification**
- Responsible for filtering toxins from blood
- Includes drugs and other harmful substances

**3. Bile Production**
- Produces bile
- Helps break down and digest fats in small intestine

**4. Storage**
- Stores excess glucose in form of glycogen
- Stores vitamins, minerals, other nutrients

**5. Immune Function**
- Plays role in immune function
- Helps remove bacteria and foreign substances from bloodstream

**6. Hormone Regulation**
- Helps regulate hormone levels
- Produces and breaks down hormones
- Includes insulin and various sex hormones

#### Summary

The liver is a highly complex organ with wide range of functions essential to maintaining overall health and well-being.
